About Northern Numbers

Free, accurate calculators built specifically for Canadians.

Northern Numbers is a Canadian-built resource for practical financial planning. It was created by a small team of developers, designers, and finance-minded contributors who wanted to make everyday money questions easier to answer without relying on generic U.S.-style calculators or confusing spreadsheets. The goal was simple: give Canadians a more trustworthy way to understand their financial choices using rules that actually apply here.

The need for this site came from a very common frustration. Many online calculators are either too simplistic, too vague, or built for the wrong country. Canadian tax rules, mortgage compounding, TFSA room calculations, RRSP contribution limits, FHSA eligibility, CPP timing, and OAS clawbacks all work differently than what many generic calculators assume. Northern Numbers was created to close that gap by building financial tools with Canadian context from the start.

We focus on calculators that people actually use in real life. Whether you are buying a home, comparing loan options, planning for retirement, trying to understand your tax bill, or deciding how much to save for the future, our tools are designed to be straightforward and useful. The experience is intentionally simple: enter the numbers you already know, and the calculator helps you work through the rest without requiring a finance degree or a long learning curve.

Our calculator lineup continues to grow because financial planning is not one-size-fits-all. You can find tools for mortgages, TFSA growth, RRSP planning, FHSA timing, compound interest, car loans, personal loans, RESP projections, monthly budgeting, CPP, OAS, and income tax estimates. We also pay attention to the kinds of questions that matter most to Canadians in day-to-day life, including debt management, savings goals, retirement readiness, and major purchases that can shape household finances for years.

Accuracy is at the heart of everything we do. Every calculator on this site is built with Canadian rules in mind and reviewed against official sources such as the Canada Revenue Agency, Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation, the Bank of Canada, Employment and Social Development Canada, and the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions. When contribution limits, rates, or tax rules change, we update the calculations so the results remain relevant and useful. We do not treat financial planning as a guessing game, and we do not assume that a tool designed for one country will work well for another.

We also believe that good financial tools should be transparent. There is no sign-up wall, no subscription requirement, and no need to hand over personal information just to run a calculation. The calculations live in your browser, and we are clear about the assumptions used in each tool. When a calculator depends on a specific tax rule or government threshold, we make that context easy to find instead of burying it in technical language.

The purpose of Northern Numbers is not to replace professional advice. It is to give Canadians a dependable starting point for understanding their own numbers. A calculator can help you compare options, test assumptions, and see what might happen under different scenarios. That kind of clarity is useful whether you are making a small decision or planning a major financial move that could affect your household for years to come.

We are committed to improving the site over time. New calculators are added carefully, existing ones are reviewed regularly, and the content is updated as our understanding deepens. If you use a tool and notice something that looks off, we want to hear about it. Great financial tools are built through iteration, and our goal is to earn trust by being accurate, practical, and transparent in everything we publish.

Northern Numbers is meant to be a practical companion for everyday financial decisions. From the first mortgage estimate to the final retirement projection, the site exists to make Canada-specific planning feel less intimidating and more usable. We believe that when people can understand their numbers clearly, they are better equipped to make thoughtful decisions about debt, savings, taxes, and long-term goals.

Trusted Canadian Sources

Every calculator on Northern Numbers is built and verified against official Canadian sources. We update our data annually when CRA and government rates are published.

  • 🇨🇦 Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) — Tax brackets, TFSA limits, RRSP limits, FHSA rules, RESP/CESG, CPP and EI rates
  • 🏠 Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) — Mortgage insurance premiums, stress test rates, amortization rules
  • 🏦 Bank of Canada — Inflation targets, historical rate data, monetary policy
  • 📋 Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) — OAS, GIS, CPP benefit amounts, EI premium rates
  • 🏛 Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions (OSFI) — Mortgage stress test qualifying rates
