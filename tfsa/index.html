Understanding Your TFSA

What is TFSA Contribution Room?

Your TFSA contribution room is the maximum amount you can deposit into all your TFSAs combined. It accumulates every year starting at age 18 (as long as you're a Canadian resident with a SIN), regardless of whether you actually have a TFSA open. If you were 18 or older in 2009, your lifetime contribution room in 2026 is $95,000.

Annual TFSA Limits

The CRA sets a new annual TFSA limit each year, indexed to inflation and rounded to the nearest $500. The limit has been $7,000 for 2024, 2025, and 2026. That new room is added to your account on January 1 each year. Any unused room from previous years carries forward indefinitely.

Withdrawals and Re-contributions

When you withdraw from your TFSA, that amount is added back to your contribution room — but not until January 1 of the following year. If you withdraw and re-contribute in the same calendar year without having the room available, you'll be over-contributing and face a 1% per month penalty on the excess amount from the CRA.

Over-Contribution Risk

The CRA charges a tax equal to 1% per month on the highest excess TFSA amount for every month the excess remains. This penalty continues until the excess is withdrawn. Because CRA My Account may not show current-year contributions until the following spring, you should always track your own contributions carefully and verify with your financial institution.
